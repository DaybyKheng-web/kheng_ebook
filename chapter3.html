Chapter III: A Caucus-Race and a Long Tale

  • ← Previous Chapter

They were indeed a queer-looking party that assembled on the bank—the birds with wet feathers, the animals with damp fur, and all of them dripping, uncomfortable, and cross.

The first concern was how to get dry again. After a short discussion, the Mouse, who seemed like a leader, called out, “Sit down, all of you, and listen to me! I’ll soon make you dry enough!”

They sat in a ring as the Mouse began a dull history lecture about William the Conqueror. It didn’t help. Alice remained soaking wet.

The Dodo then proposed something different. “A Caucus-race!” it said. Without explanation, the race began, and everyone ran whenever they liked, stopping when they chose. After a while, the Dodo declared, “Everybody has won, and all must have prizes.”

Alice, caught off guard, handed out a box of comfits she happened to have. Everyone got one, and after the chaos of eating them, they sat again in a ring and asked the Mouse to tell its story.

“Mine is a long and a sad tale,” said the Mouse, turning to Alice. She looked down at its tail, thinking it meant a physical tail, and stayed puzzled as the Mouse began its tale in verse.

“Fury said to a mouse, That he met in the house, Let us go to law: I will prosecute you...”

But Alice wasn’t paying attention, and the Mouse noticed. It was insulted and stormed off. Alice and the others called after it, but it didn’t return.

Trying to lighten the mood, Alice mentioned her cat Dinah again. The reaction was instant—birds flew away, animals scattered, and she was left alone once more, feeling very sad and missing home.
